Pros and Cons:
Corporate Production or Development Perspective

Marc Andreessen tweeted: "The best minds of my generation are thinking about how to make people buy support contracts for free software." --Anonymous

Con: Less Income Potential

A lot of work will result in a low return.

Other methods of income might need to be used (eg. Dropbox, RedHat).

Can lead to embarrassment for your company if the quality is poor.

Con: Security Leaks

People with malicious intents may find security problems easier to exploit them.

Uber recently pushed secret passwords in plaintext to their GitHub.

Bots scrap GitHub and other code bases for secrets all the time.

Pro: Good for Reputation and Community at Large

Going open source is good for reputation and seen as giving back to the community.

Open source projects create great communities and open many possibilities for collaboration between businesses and the OS community.

Pro: Innovation, Quality and Big Brains

Many open source projects are at the forefront of innovation and possess many cutting edge features that release sooner than proprietary software.

Popular OSS are often of equal or greater quality than their commercial counterpart.

Most of the greatest minds in programming contribute or have contributed to open source. (Linus Torvalds, Ken Thompson, etc)

Corporate Production or Development Summary

  • Best determined on a case by case basis.
  • Important to find other revenues of income.
  • Security might be a deciding factor when working with sensitive information.
